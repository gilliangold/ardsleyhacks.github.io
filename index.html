About

Ardsley Hacks is the first hackathon Ardsley High School is hosting. High schoolers, of any experience level, can participate. You can form teams of 1-5 students, even across different schools, and work to make projects relating to coding. We'll have workshops and mentors to help you with your projects and learn! We will announce the surprise theme a week before the competition, after which you can start coding, and continue until 4 pm on Saturday. Written Code

This category is for people who have the best written code. Code should be readable, well-structured, well-organized, reliable, and efficient.

Overall Project

This catergory is for having the best project as a whole. Project should be the most innovative and ready to use. Simpply the best!

Beginner Category

This category is for those who have not been to any hackathons. We welcome coders of all experiences.

F.A.Q.

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where teams get together and build a project relating to coding, using software or hardware components, in a short period of time.


Who can come to Ardsley Hacks?

Anyone! Everyone is welcome no matter their prior coding knowledge. Hackers must be high schoolers and bring one adult chaperone from their school for insurance purposes.


Is there a theme for Ardsley Hacks?

We will be announcing the theme a week before the Hackathon. When we announce the theme, teams are encouraged to begin working on their projects.
